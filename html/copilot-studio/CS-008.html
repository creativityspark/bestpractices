Plan your deployment channels carefully and design the conversational experience to match each channel's capabilities.

1
Guideline 1
Identify and prioritize the target channels (Microsoft Teams, web chat, mobile, custom) before designing the conversation flow.
2
Guideline 2
Test the agent on every target channel before going live - message formatting, adaptive cards, and authentication behave differently across channels.
3
Guideline 3
Write a clear and helpful greeting message that sets expectations about what the agent can do.
4
Guideline 4
Use quick replies and suggested actions to guide users through common paths and reduce free-text input.
5
Guideline 5
Keep messages concise - avoid walls of text, and split long responses across multiple messages when necessary.
1
Rationale 1
Conversation flows designed for web chat may not render correctly in Teams or other channels due to differences in card support, message length, and authentication.
2
Rationale 2
Testing on the actual channel prevents formatting issues, broken links, or authentication failures from reaching production users.
3
Rationale 3
A well-crafted greeting reduces fallback rates by immediately telling users what the agent can help with.
4
Rationale 4
Quick replies and suggested actions lower the barrier to interaction and reduce the chance of misunderstood free-text input.
5
Rationale 5
Long, unbroken messages are difficult to read in chat interfaces and lead to users skipping important information.
